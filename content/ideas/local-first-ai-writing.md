## Idea

A post exploring the philosophy and practice of local-first AI-assisted writing. Why keeping your source files as plain Markdown, using local or API-based AI agents for assistance, and owning your publishing pipeline is better than relying on all-in-one cloud platforms.

## Why Now

With AI writing tools proliferating (Notion AI, Lex, Jasper, etc.), there's a growing lock-in risk. Writers are trading ownership for convenience. This post argues you can have both.

## Target Reader

Writers who use or are curious about AI writing tools but care about owning their content. Developers who want to build their own writing pipeline.

## Research Questions

- What are the pain points of cloud-only AI writing platforms?
- What local-first AI tools exist today (Ollama, LM Studio, etc.)?
- How does the agent-based workflow compare to all-in-one solutions?
- What are real examples of writers moving back to local-first?

## Initial Thoughts

The key insight is separating assistance from ownership. AI agents can help at every stage (research, outline, draft, edit) while the source stays in portable Markdown. This is the opposite of platforms where your writing is trapped in a database.
