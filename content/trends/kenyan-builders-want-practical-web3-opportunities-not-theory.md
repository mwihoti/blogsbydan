More Kenyan developers and founders are interested in blockchain, but many are tired of abstract Web3 conversations that do not lead to real products, revenue, or career opportunities. Programmes like MiniHack are shifting attention toward practical building: smart contracts, USDC payments, embedded wallets, bounties, MVPs, and real business use cases on Avalanche. The opportunity is to show builders that Web3 is not just speculation; it can be used to ship useful tools for Kenyan SMEs, communities, and startups.
